Thrissur

It is believed that the word Thrissur is derived from the word “Thrissivaperur”. The city of Thrissur has a structure that slopes down to the sides from the Vadakkunnath Kshetra on the high ground in the center of the town. Trisshivaperurpatnam, steeped in antiquity, was known as 'Vrishabhadripuram' and 'Thenkailasam' in ancient times. Thrissur has played an important role in the political history of South India since ancient times. The ancient history of the city is intertwined with the history of the Sanghakalachera dynasty, which was based in Vanchinad and ruled most of Kerala. Entire parts of present day Thrissur were part of Chera Empire at that time. Later, when the last Perumal princely states were divided, Thrissur became part of the Kochi kingdom (Perumpadap Swarupam). The district can claim to have played a vital role in fostering trade relations between Kerala and the outside world during ancient and medieval times. Being the port center of the district, Kodungallur has been a prominent commercial and marketing center since ancient times. This country also has its own tradition of sheltering all three communities, Christians, Jews and Muslims, who later played a role in the growth of Malabar. Cheramanpally, which is believed to be the first Muslim mosque in India, is located in Kodungallur. Thrissur is famous for its Palayoor pilgrimage center, which is said to have been founded by Saint Thomas Slieha, and Azhikode Marthommapalli.

Main Attractions

Athirappilly Water Falls

Athirapilly Falls is situated in Athirapilly Panchayat in Chalakudy Taluk of Thrissur District in Kerala, India on the Chalakudy River, which originates from the upper reaches of the Western Ghats at the entrance to the Sholayar ranges. It is the largest waterfall in Kerala, which stands tall at 81.5 feet.

Sree Vadakkumnathan Temple

The Vadakkumnathan Temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva in Thrissur, in the Thrissur district of Kerala, India. The temple is a classical example of the architectural style of Kerala and has one monumental tower on each of the four sides in addition to a koothambalam.
